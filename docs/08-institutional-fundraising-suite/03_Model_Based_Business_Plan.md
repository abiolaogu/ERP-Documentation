# Model-Based Business Plan

## 1. Strategic Objective

Build the leading technology-driven operating platform for high-friction enterprise workflows, with defensible economics, enterprise-grade governance, and repeatable expansion.

## 2. Business Model Design

### Revenue engines

1. Subscription platform licenses (recurring)
2. Usage-based API and automation processing
3. High-value implementation services (kept constrained)

### Margin design principles

- Keep services at <=5% of revenue to avoid margin dilution.
- Improve gross margin through infrastructure efficiency and support tooling.
- Maintain a product-heavy value proposition to support premium ACV growth.

## 3. Customer and Segment Model

| Segment | Employee Range | Typical ACV | Sales Motion | Target Share of New ARR |
|---|---:|---:|---|---:|
| Mid-market | 500-2,000 | $70k-$120k | inside + partner-assisted | 35% |
| Enterprise | 2,000-10,000 | $120k-$300k | direct field + solution engineering | 55% |
| Strategic | >10,000 | $300k+ | executive-led account strategy | 10% |

## 4. Growth Architecture

### Demand generation stack

- Account-based outbound for high-probability enterprise accounts
- Content and thought leadership for inbound efficiency
- Partner-originated opportunities from implementation ecosystems

### Conversion funnel assumptions (base case)

| Funnel Stage | Conversion |
|---|---:|
| MQL -> SQL | 42% |
| SQL -> Qualified opportunity | 55% |
| Qualified opportunity -> Closed-won | 26% |

`Effective MQL-to-win conversion = 42% x 55% x 26% = 6.0%`

## 6. Operating Model

### Product and engineering

- Quarterly releases tied to monetizable roadmap items
- Reliability SLOs embedded into release gating
- Security and compliance controls integrated into SDLC

### Customer success

- 30/60/90-day onboarding plans
- Executive business reviews for expansion triggers
- Usage health scoring and proactive risk intervention

## 7. Financial Operating Plan

### Revenue build

- Starting base + new ARR + expansion ARR - churn ARR
- Expansion driven by module attach and usage intensity

### Expense build

- R&D tied to roadmap and platform reliability scope
- S&M tied to customer acquisition and pipeline targets
- G&A scaled with governance requirements and geographic expansion

## 9. Board-Level KPI Framework

| Category | KPI | Target Direction |
|---|---|---|
| Growth | New ARR | Up and right with quota attainment discipline |
| Efficiency | CAC payback | Declining toward <=15 months |
| Quality | Gross margin | Expanding toward 80%+ |
| Retention | NRR | Sustained above 120% |
| Predictability | Forecast accuracy | +/-10% quarterly variance |

## 10. Governance and Control Structure

- Monthly operating review (finance, sales, product, engineering)
- Quarterly strategic review (board and key investors)
- Risk and compliance committee with documented mitigation tracking
- Rolling 18-month scenario planning and runway monitoring

## 11. Value Creation Bridge

Institutional value creation is expected through:

1. Revenue scale with healthy retention and expansion mechanics
2. Margin expansion from productization and support leverage
3. Risk reduction from governance and compliance maturity
4. Strategic optionality via platform relevance in category consolidation

This business plan is designed to produce both growth and quality, supporting durable valuation outcomes rather than short-term top-line inflation.
