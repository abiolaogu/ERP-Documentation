# Investor FAQ (20 Difficult Questions and Institutional Answers)

## 1) Why is this a venture-scale outcome versus a profitable niche business?

The model combines large market scope, recurring subscription economics, and expansion mechanics (NRR >120% target). This supports compounding ARR and margin leverage beyond niche software trajectories.

## 2) What proves this is not a feature, but a platform?

The product is a multi-layer system: user workspace, admin governance plane, API and service orchestration, and auditable telemetry. Customers adopt multiple modules and expand usage across org units.

## 3) Why will incumbents not crush pricing?

Incumbents can discount but cannot quickly replicate speed-to-value plus governance depth in one deployment model. Our win strategy is value density and deployment speed, not price parity.

## 4) How resilient is demand in a downturn?

We sell into cost-out and risk-control budgets. Our value proposition is cycle-time reduction, audit readiness, and operational efficiency, which are prioritized in constrained macro environments.

## 5) What is the evidence that CAC improves over time?

Modeled CAC reduction is supported by channel mix shift toward partner and expansion-led ARR, stronger conversion from verticalized messaging, and improved brand-led inbound quality.

## 6) What if NRR drops below 110%?

We have a contingency plan: reduce new-logo spend pace, focus on onboarding quality and module attach, and prioritize retention economics before re-accelerating acquisition.

## 7) What are the top three model sensitivities?

ACV growth, win rate, and gross margin trajectory. These are explicitly modeled in downside/base/upside scenarios and reviewed monthly in variance governance.

## 8) Why is services revenue capped at 5%?

To preserve software gross margin profile and avoid becoming implementation-heavy. Services are used as strategic accelerators, not the core revenue engine.

## 9) What protects gross margin expansion?

Architecture choices (event-driven services, autoscaling), support tooling, and implementation standardization reduce delivery cost per customer as scale increases.

## 10) How defensible is your data advantage?

Workflow telemetry and outcome data improve recommendations and benchmark intelligence over time. This creates a reinforcement loop that is hard for point tools to replicate.

## 11) How concentrated is customer risk?

Commercial policy caps top-customer ARR concentration and requires diversification by segment and geography. Concentration is monitored in board-level KPI reporting.

## 12) What is the path to EBITDA positive?

Revenue growth with improving gross margin and controlled opex growth yields EBITDA-positive profile in Y4 in the base case, with spend-gate protections under downside.

## 13) What does success look like 24 months after this round?

Durable enterprise GTM repeatability, NRR >120%, CAC payback approaching <=15 months, and compliance maturity that broadens procurement access.

## 14) How do you govern model risk in AI-assisted workflows?

Policy controls, human override points, audit logs, and performance monitoring are built into service-layer governance. High-risk actions require explicit approval pathways.

## 15) How do you manage security and regulatory risk?

Through defense-in-depth controls, regular testing, incident readiness drills, and control-evidence automation aligned to enterprise and regulatory requirements.

## 16) What is the board governance model?

Monthly operating reviews, quarterly strategy reviews, formal risk committee tracking, and metric definitions locked in a controlled KPI dictionary.

## 17) Why is this the right amount of capital to raise?

$25M funds the exact step-up from early repeatability to institutional scale while preserving discipline. It is milestone-gated and avoids both undercapitalization and excess dilution.

## 18) What are credible exit paths?

Strategic M&A, late-stage private scale with growth equity, and potential IPO. The company is managed to maximize optionality, not to force a single path.

## 19) What return profile should investors underwrite?

Modeled 5-year outcomes: 4.3x MOIC downside, 9.4x base, 17.3x upside, with IRR ranges of 34%-76% depending on execution and market multiple regime.

## 20) What is the single biggest reason this could fail?

Execution mismatch between GTM pace and product/reliability maturity. Mitigation is strict milestone-gated spending, release governance, and operating KPI discipline.
