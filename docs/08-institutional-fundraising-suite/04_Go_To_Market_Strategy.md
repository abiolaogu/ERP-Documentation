# Go-To-Market Strategy (Tactical and Financially Modeled)

## 1. GTM Objective

Scale from early repeatability to institutional-grade commercial engine with predictable pipeline, efficient acquisition, and expansion-led ARR growth.

## 2. ICP Prioritization and Territory Design

### ICP scoring dimensions

- Workflow complexity and urgency
- Regulatory and audit burden
- Data/process fragmentation
- Budget authority concentration
- Integration surface area

### Territory model

| Territory | Focus Verticals | Account Target | Sales Pod Structure |
|---|---|---:|---|
| US East | financial services, healthcare | 220 | 1 AE + 1 SE + 1 CSM |
| US West | technology, logistics | 210 | 1 AE + 1 SE + 1 CSM |
| UK | regulated services | 120 | 1 AE + shared SE + partner CSM |
| DACH | industrial and pharma ops | 90 | partner-led + overlay AE |

## 3. Channel Strategy

| Channel | Role | Cost Profile | Scale Profile |
|---|---|---|---|
| Direct enterprise sales | large logo acquisition | high fixed + variable | high ACV, lower velocity |
| Partner-assisted | implementation leverage and trust transfer | moderate variable | medium-high velocity |
| Inbound/PLG-assist | efficient mid-market conversion | lower CAC | high velocity, lower ACV |

Target channel mix by new ARR (Y3):

- Direct: 55%
- Partner: 30%
- Inbound/PLG-assist: 15%

## 4. Demand Generation Program

### Program architecture

1. Account-based campaigns (named accounts and buying committee mapping)
2. Vertical playbooks (problem framing + quantifiable ROI)
3. Executive events and strategic proof-point narratives
4. Product content engine (use cases, calculators, deployment patterns)

### Funnel math model (Y2 planning)

| Metric | Value |
|---|---:|
| Marketing qualified leads (MQLs) | 2,400 |
| MQL -> SQL | 42% |
| SQL -> Qualified opportunity | 55% |
| Qualified opportunity -> Won | 26% |
| Expected wins | 144 |
| Average new logo ARR | $96k |
| Expected new ARR from funnel | $13.8M |

Formula:

`Wins = 2,400 x 0.42 x 0.55 x 0.26 = 144`

`New ARR = 144 x $96k = $13.8M`

## 6. Pricing and Packaging Strategy

### Package tiers

- Core: workflow foundation + standard admin controls
- Growth: advanced automations + integrations + analytics
- Enterprise: policy governance, custom controls, premium support

### Pricing levers

- Base platform fee
- User band pricing
- Usage units for automation/API volume
- Enterprise control module add-ons

Pricing discipline rule: any discount >15% requires CFO and CRO sign-off with expansion plan documented.

## 7. Land-and-Expand Program

| Expansion Lever | Trigger Signal | Expected Impact |
|---|---|---|
| Additional modules | >70% workflow saturation in current scope | +20-35% ARR per account |
| API usage growth | sustained automation throughput increase | +10-25% ARR |
| Geographic/BU rollout | successful first business unit deployment | 1.8x-2.6x account ARR |

Expansion target: >=40% of annual new ARR equivalent from existing base by Y4.

## 8. CAC and Payback Model

### Blended CAC model

`CAC = (Sales + Marketing spend allocated to acquisition) / New logos`

| Metric | Y1 | Y2 | Y3 |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Acquisition spend ($M) | 7.2 | 12.5 | 20.0 |
| New logos | 45 | 86 | 160 |
| Blended CAC ($k) | 160 | 145 | 125 |

### CAC payback model

`CAC Payback (months) = CAC / ((ACV x Gross Margin) / 12)`

Y3 example:

`= 125,000 / ((117,000 x 0.80) / 12) = 16.0 months`

## 9. Partner Strategy

### Partner archetypes

- Implementation consultancies
- Industry-specialist boutiques
- Platform ecosystem connectors

### Partner economics

- Referral fees: 8-12% first-year ACV
- Co-sell accelerators for strategic verticals
- Certification model to maintain delivery quality

## 10. Governance and Operating Cadence

- Weekly pipeline inspection: stage hygiene and deal risk scoring
- Monthly CAC and conversion diagnostics by channel
- Quarterly territory and quota recalibration
- Closed-loop feedback from customer success into product and pricing

## 11. Financial Link to 5-Year Model

- GTM spend trajectory maps directly to S&M line in financial model.
- Funnel conversion assumptions map to revenue ramp.
- CAC improvement trajectory drives FCF inflection.
- Expansion conversion assumptions drive NRR and valuation durability.

This GTM strategy is intentionally engineered as a measurable system, not a marketing narrative.
