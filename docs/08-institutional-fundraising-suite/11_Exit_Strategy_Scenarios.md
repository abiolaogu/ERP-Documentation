# Exit Strategy Scenarios

## 1. Exit Philosophy

The company is built for strategic relevance and durable unit economics, supporting both strategic M&A and public-market readiness. Exit planning is scenario-based and tied to objective milestones.

## 2. Exit Pathways

### Path A: Strategic Acquisition

Potential acquirers:

- Enterprise software platforms consolidating workflow and governance categories
- Infrastructure and operations software firms seeking application-layer expansion
- Large data/AI platforms requiring enterprise workflow distribution

### Path B: Growth Equity + Pre-IPO Scale

- Continue private scaling with late-stage institutional capital
- Improve predictability, governance, and free cash flow profile
- Position for public listing when scale and rule-of-40 trajectory are durable

### Path C: IPO

- Viable if revenue scale, growth quality, and compliance maturity meet public thresholds
- Requires strong internal controls, audit discipline, and operating consistency

## 5. Milestones that De-Risk Exit Optionality

| Milestone | Exit Relevance |
|---|---|
| Sustained NRR >=120% | supports premium growth-quality multiple |
| EBITDA inflection to positive | increases strategic and financial buyer confidence |
| Enterprise logo depth | validates category relevance and reduces concentration risk |
| Security/compliance maturity | removes diligence friction for strategic or IPO path |
| Forecast accuracy and governance | improves credibility in late-stage and public markets |

## 6. Buyer Diligence Readiness

- Product and technical diligence
- Commercial quality of revenue diligence
- Security and compliance diligence
- Financial control and forecast discipline diligence

## 7. Recommendation

Manage the business to maximize optionality rather than forcing a single exit route. This means preserving growth while building quality metrics and governance standards that can withstand strategic and public market scrutiny.
